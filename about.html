He is a Royal College certified emergency physician and an ordained, certified mohel through the Rabbinical Assembly of the Conservative Movement — a combination that very few practitioners anywhere can claim.

His path to medicine began long before medical school. A deep commitment to science, to people, and to the communities he serves led him to McMaster University, where he completed his medical degree in 2020. He subsequently undertook a rigorous five-year residency in emergency medicine at the University of Ottawa, developing additional expertise in sport and exercise medicine. Throughout his medical training, he pursued formal instruction in neonatal circumcision, and his expertise in this area has been recognized by the College of Physicians and Surgeons of Ontario.

Alongside his medical career, he has remained deeply connected to his Jewish heritage and to the Conservative movement. He completed the BRIT KODESH course — a comprehensive training program for aspiring mohalim — and was subsequently ordained and certified by the Rabbinical Assembly to perform ritual circumcision in full accordance with Conservative halacha. For him, performing a bris milah is not simply a procedure; it is an honour and a privilege.
