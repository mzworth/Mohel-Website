What Is Bris Milah?

Bris milah — also written brit milah — is the Jewish covenant of circumcision, one of the oldest and most central commandments in Jewish law. The word bris means covenant, and milah refers to circumcision. Together, the term describes both the physical act and the profound spiritual dimension it carries: the welcoming of a Jewish boy into the covenant between God and the Jewish people, first established with Abraham in the book of Genesis.

The commandment to perform bris milah on the eighth day of a boy's life is among the most widely observed mitzvot in Jewish practice, maintained across generations and across every stream of Jewish tradition.
